## Adoption is part of the architecture

An unused AI system creates no business value.

Adoption depends on relevance, trust, ease of use, workflow fit, leadership support, training, incentives, support and feedback.

## User segmentation

Different users require different approaches: early adopters, operational users, managers, subject-matter experts, risk teams and executives.

## Trust-building design

Users are more likely to trust AI when they can see sources, confidence, limitations, reasoning factors, approval state and an escalation route.

## Change network

Create executive sponsor, product owner, business champions, local super-users, support team and feedback forum.

## Training

Training should cover purpose, approved use, limitations, prompting, data handling, verification, escalation and feedback.

## Adoption metrics

Track active users, repeat usage, task completion, time saved, acceptance rate, override rate, escalation rate, user satisfaction and business benefit.

## Case study: financial-services assistant

Ship inside the existing service-desk UI, not a separate portal. Show citations by default. Train on “verify before send” for regulated product answers. Champions in each contact-centre pod; weekly feedback forum for false or stale answers.

## Common failure modes

- Portal nobody opens
- No escalation path when AI is wrong
- Training as a one-off slide deck
- Measuring launches instead of repeat usage
- Ignoring override and escalation signals

## Solution Engineer checklist

<div className="ase-checklist">

- [ ] Segments and journeys defined

## Practical exercise

Interview five target users. Ask what would make them ignore the tool after week one. Convert each answer into a design or change action.
