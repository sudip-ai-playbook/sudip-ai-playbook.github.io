# Business Learning

<ExecSummary>
Business Learning is the practice pack for Stage 1 capability: brief an AI solution as a business proposition, speak the buyer’s language, communicate for decisions, and build industry fluency with a workable Excel workbook.
</ExecSummary>

<WhenToUse>
- Use alongside [Stage 1: Business and consulting](../stage-1-business-consulting) when you need depth and reusable artefacts, not only topic summaries.
- Start with the overview below, then work the pages in order — finish each week’s outputs in the Excel workbook.
- Pair with [Business Fundamentals](../01-business-fundamentals), [Industry and Domain Knowledge](../03-industry-domain-knowledge) and [Communication and Executive Articulation](../28-communication-executive-articulation).
</WhenToUse>

## What is in this subsection

| Page | What you get |
| --- | --- |
| [Business Briefing](./business-briefing) | Full business and product blueprint for enterprise customer-service AI (NovaAssist / NovaConnect) |
| [Business Keywords](./business-keywords) | Glossary of economics, finance, strategy and commercial terms |
| [Business Power Words](./business-power-words) | Executive communication structure, leadership verbs and commercial phrases |
| [Domain Knowledge Gathering](./domain-knowledge-gathering) | Seven-part industry learning system and 10-hour sprint method |
| [Domain Knowledge workbook](./domain-knowledge-workbook) | Downloadable, workable Excel — skill matrix, 12-week plan, industry sprints, battlecards, terms, use cases and evidence register |

## Recommended path

1. Skim [Business Keywords](./business-keywords) so the vocabulary is available when you need it.
2. Study [Business Power Words](./business-power-words) and practise one five-minute recommendation-led briefing.
3. Read [Domain Knowledge Gathering](./domain-knowledge-gathering), then download the [workbook](./domain-knowledge-workbook).
4. Run one 10-hour industry sprint in the workbook (sheets 04–08).
5. Use [Business Briefing](./business-briefing) as the gold-standard example of a complete business and product proposition.
