# Project Management

<ExecSummary>
A structured study path based on the Project Management Institute (PMI) *A Guide to the Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK® Guide) – Fifth Edition*: what a project is, how process groups and knowledge areas fit together, and how to apply them on AI and enterprise delivery.
</ExecSummary>

<WhenToUse>
- You are preparing for PMP® / CAPM® style framing, or aligning a delivery team on a shared vocabulary.
- You need a map of the ten knowledge areas and five process groups without reading the full official guide first.
- You want practice artefacts (charter, WBS thinking, risk register, stakeholder engagement) you can reuse on client work.
</WhenToUse>

## Why this category exists

Commercial and AI programmes fail less often when scope, schedule, cost, risk, and stakeholders are managed with a **shared language**. The PMBOK® Guide Fifth Edition is that language for many enterprises. This category is a **study and practice map**—original summaries and exercises—not a substitute for PMI’s copyrighted publication.

## Start here

1. **[PMBOK® Guide Fifth Edition — study map](./pmbok-fifth-edition)** — chapters 1–13, process groups, and how the knowledge areas connect.
2. Pair with Learning Map **[Stage 5 — Commercial & delivery](../stage-5-commercial-delivery)** and **[Delivery & programme management](../25-delivery-programme-management)**.

## What you will practise

| Focus | Typical artefacts |
| --- | --- |
| Integration | Charter, integrated plan, change control |
| Scope | Requirements, WBS, baseline, validation |
| Time & cost | Schedule model, EVM-style tracking |
| Quality & risk | Quality plan, risk register, responses |
| People & stakeholders | RACI, communication plan, engagement matrix |
| Procurement | Make-or-buy, SOW, contract control |
