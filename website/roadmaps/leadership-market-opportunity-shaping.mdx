# Market Opportunity Shaping Roadmap

Shaping major markets is different from pursuing pipeline. This roadmap turns the full leadership guide into an ordered journey from external signal to scalable commercial position—before clients issue a narrowly framed RFP.

> Change → Problem → Point of view → Proposition → Proof → Scale

<ExecSummary>
Sense external change, form a testable market hypothesis, choose where you have a right to win, publish a distinctive point of view, build proposition layers with staged investment, mobilise accounts and partners, and track leading indicators of market position—not only late revenue.
</ExecSummary>

<WhenToUse>
- Use when creating or renewing a major market category (responsible AI, AI transformation, assurance of AI, sector regulatory response)—not only an individual deal.

## Capability map

Ten elements of a durable market position:

1. Significant external change
2. Clearly defined client problem
3. Compelling point of view
4. Relevant capabilities
5. Credible evidence
6. Commercial proposition
7. Route to market
8. Scalable delivery model
9. Trusted ecosystem
10. Visible leadership

## Outside-in vs inside-out

| Approach | Starts with | Typical failure |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Inside-out** | Accelerator, team or partnership | Impressive asset, weak client urgency |
| **Outside-in** | Regulatory, tech, economic, geopolitical, social or industry shift | Clear executive problem and right-to-win filter |

## Opportunity selection scorecard

Score each candidate market before major investment:

| Dimension | Ask |
| --- | --- |
| Strategic importance | Fits priority industries, capabilities, accounts, regions? |
| Client urgency | Linked to revenue, cost, risk, compliance, resilience or advantage? |
| Market size | Project, series of projects, or long-term service market? |
| Timing | Too early, correctly timed, or already late? |
| Right to win | Industry, tech, regulatory, IP, relationships, brand? |
| Differentiation | Meaningfully different and hard to copy? |
| Scalability | Repeatable methods vs bespoke every time? |
| Risk | Legal, ethical, reputational, operational, investment? |
| Investment | People, tech, partners, research, sales required? |
| Cross-service potential | Connects multiple firm capabilities? |

## Staged investment

| Stage | Purpose | Typical activities | Exit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1. Explore** | Is the problem real? | Interviews, regulatory review, roundtables, early prototypes | Validated signal |
| **2. Validate** | Will clients pay? | Paid diagnostics, workshops, pilots, lighthouse deals | Paid evidence |
| **3. Productise** | Can we repeat? | Methods, pricing, playbooks, accelerators, training | Repeatable offer |
| **4. Scale** | Can we grow? | Dedicated leadership, hubs, managed services, alliances | Multi-account growth |
| **5. Renew** | Still distinctive? | Refresh POV, assets, partnerships | Continued relevance |

## Fourteen-stage journey

### Stage 1 — Detect the change

Map regulatory, technological, economic, geopolitical, social and industry shifts.

**Build:** signal log with sources and strength.

### Stage 2 — Define affected clients

Name sectors, functions and organisation types hit first and hardest.

**Build:** client impact map.

### Stage 3 — Articulate the problem

Write the issue in clear executive language (not technology slogans).

**Build:** one-page problem statement.

### Stage 4 — Form a hypothesis

State what clients will need and why current approaches fail.

**Build:** market hypothesis (signal → hypothesis → potential market → leadership action).

### Stage 5 — Test with the market

Interviews, workshops, roundtables, research and small paid engagements.

**Exit:** Evidence clients care enough to invest attention or money.

### Stage 6 — Select a priority position

Choose where market size and right to win both exist; defer the rest.

**Build:** scored opportunity shortlist (two or three max).

### Stage 7 — Develop the point of view

What is changing, what leaders misunderstand, what to do, what success looks like.

**Build:** POV statement usable in executive briefings.

### Stage 8 — Build the proposition

Services, outcomes, methods, commercial model, differentiation, expansion path.

**Build:** proposition canvas and layered offer menu.

### Stage 9 — Create proof

Research, demonstrators, benchmarks, case studies, lighthouse clients.

**Build:** proof pack tied to the POV (not tech demos for their own sake).

### Stage 10 — Build capability

Recruit, train, partner, acquire; create reusable assets and quality controls.

**Build:** capability gap plan with build / partner / buy choices.

### Stage 11 — Mobilise accounts

Playbooks, qualification questions, briefing packs, demo scripts, incentives.

**Exit:** Priority account teams can start the conversation consistently.

### Stage 12 — Scale delivery

Governance, capacity, hubs, managed-service design, risk ownership.

**Build:** delivery operating model and named RACI.

### Stage 13 — Measure results

Track awareness, demand, capability, commercial, client outcomes and strategic position.

**Build:** market dashboard (leading + lagging indicators).

### Stage 14 — Refresh the position

Re-test timing, differentiation and commercial attractiveness as the market moves.

**Exit:** Explicit renew, narrow, scale or stop decision.

## 90-day mobilisation agenda

| Window | Focus |
| --- | --- |
| **Days 1–30** | Signals, interviews, competitor view, right to win, two–three hypotheses, sponsor, success measures |
| **Days 31–60** | Client tests, roundtables, POV, initial proposition, gaps, lighthouse candidates, investment case, ownership |
| **Days 61–90** | Narrative launch, account training, lighthouse starts, assessment tools, pricing, market dashboard, 12-month plan |

## Failure modes to catch early

| Failure | Correction |
| --- | --- |
| Following every trend | Score and limit priority markets |
| Starting with a solution | Outside-in problem first |
| Generic thought leadership | Distinctive, evidence-based POV |
| Visibility without influence | Convene + commercial journey |
| Campaign without delivery | Capability before scale claims |
| Internal fragmentation | One market owner and messaging |
| Weak commercial model | Entry service + expansion + recurring path |
| Logo partnerships | Shared proposition, economics, quality |
| Measuring activity | Outcomes and position indicators |
| Fear-based demand | Proportionate, balanced framing |

## Evidence of readiness

Before claiming a market is “shaped”:

- [ ] Outside-in change and affected clients documented
- [ ] Market hypothesis tested with senior clients
- [ ] Distinctive POV published and used by account teams
- [ ] Layered proposition with commercial model
- [ ] Staged investment decision (explore / validate / productise / scale)
- [ ] Proof pack (research, demo, lighthouse or benchmark)
- [ ] Named market, proposition, delivery and risk owners
- [ ] Account mobilisation pack live for priority teams
- [ ] Market dashboard with leading indicators
- [ ] Explicit link from insight → diagnostic → delivery → managed service

## Worked pattern (responsible / trusted AI)

**Signals:** GenAI experimentation; board uncertainty; regulatory attention; shadow tools; weak testing methods; third-party model risk.

**POV:** Responsible AI is an enterprise capability (strategy, governance, data, security, model risk, legal, workforce, testing, monitoring, assurance)—not a policy PDF.

**Shape:** research → convene boards/risk/regulators → maturity assessment → classification framework → red teaming / evaluation lab → control mapping → operating model → account training → alliances → lighthouse → continuous assurance managed service.

<KeyTakeaways>
- Markets are shaped before RFPs: change, problem, POV and proof beat accelerator-first selling.
- Invest in stages; scale only when clients pay and delivery can repeat.
- Thought leadership without a commercial journey is publicity; partnerships without shared economics are logos.
- One market owner, mobilised accounts and leading indicators turn an idea into a position.
</KeyTakeaways>
