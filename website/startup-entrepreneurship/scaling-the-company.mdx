# Scaling the company

<ExecSummary>
Before PMF: build a great product. After ~25 people: build a great company. Put in light structure, hire senior help, keep alignment, and manage your own psychology — the swings get worse, not better.
</ExecSummary>

<WhenToUse>
- Crossing ~20–50 employees.
- After clear product–market fit.
- When hero mode and oral culture break.
</WhenToUse>

## Ignore until then

Most of this lecture is **not** for day one. Park it for months 12–24 / ~25 people / post-PMF. Early flat “everyone → founder” is optimal — until it fails all at once around 30.

## Management

Clear reporting: one manager per person. Do not innovate on org charts. Common founder-manager failures:

1. Afraid to hire senior executives (wish they’d done it earlier).
2. **Hero mode** — stop and hire even if tickets slip for two weeks.
3. Bad delegation (bring me data, I’ll decide) → trust them to decide.
4. Personal organisation collapse — track goals and follow-ups.

Write down **how** and **why** early (wiki). Founders who wait regret it.

## HR

Not sitcom HR — structure, performance feedback, compensation bands, equity refreshes (plan ~3–5%/year for a decade). Stay ahead of vesting cliffs. Option admin software beats Excel disasters. Rules change ~50 employees. Watch burnout; full-time recruiter once things work; announce offers internally; ramp plans. Diversity of perspective early; plan careers for first 10–15 when they can’t all be VP.

## Productivity

Alignment > process theatre. Roadmap everyone can recite. All-hands, weekly staff, quarterly plans, offsites. Ship daily if needed to keep product focus. Goal: **repeatable innovation** for decade-scale excellence — not one hero release.

## Mechanics later

Books/audits, legal document collection, FF stock around B (not as seed vanity), provisional patents at ~11 months post-announce, trademarks/domains, FP&A models, tax structuring (early window), maybe an internal fundraiser vs banker dilution.

## Founder psychology

Highs higher, lows lower. From underdog to hated. Commit long-term (10 years). Vacation. Don’t lose focus to conferences/advising. Avoid acquisition sniffing unless you’d sell low. Companies die when founders quit for psychological reasons — manage that.

## Marketing / BD later

Founders own messaging and key journalist relationships — not PR flacks. Deals need great product, personal connection, competition, persistence, and asking for what you want.

## Closing image (Airbnb founder)

Find PMF by getting uncomfortably close to users. Expect launch spike → death-dip → long trough of sorrow (Airbnb ~1,000 days) → false hope wiggles → finally growth. Long and doable.

<KeyTakeaways>

- Switch job from product to company at scale.
- Light structure, senior hires, written how/why.
- Protect psychology and long-term focus.

</KeyTakeaways>
